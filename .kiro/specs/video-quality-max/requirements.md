# Requirements Document

## Introduction

This feature upgrades the AutoTube video pipeline to achieve 10/10 scores across all five quality dimensions: Script Quality, Media Quality, Narration Quality, Video Production, and YouTube Readiness. The current pipeline scores 28/50 (56%). Key upgrades include integrating Kokoro TTS for natural narration, adding background music mixing, improving media sourcing with premium fallbacks, enhancing script generation with hook-first structure, and generating complete YouTube metadata (thumbnails, descriptions, tags).

## Glossary

- **Pipeline**: The AutoTube video generation workflow consisting of Topic → Script → Media → Narration → AI Edit → Assembly → Preview stages
- **Kokoro_TTS**: A high-quality open-source text-to-speech engine that produces natural-sounding narration with prosody control, running as a local or server-side service
- **Background_Music_Mixer**: The subsystem responsible for layering ambient music underneath narration audio with ducking and volume automation
- **Hook**: The opening 30 seconds of a video script designed to capture viewer attention with a surprising statistic, provocative question, or personal-stakes statement
- **WPM**: Words per minute — the narration pacing metric; target range is 150–180 WPM for YouTube engagement
- **Ducking**: Automatic volume reduction of background music when narration is active, restoring volume during pauses
- **SEO_Metadata_Generator**: The subsystem that produces YouTube-optimized titles, descriptions, tags, and chapter markers
- **Thumbnail_Generator**: The subsystem that renders 1280×720 click-optimized thumbnail images with text overlays and emotional imagery
- **SSML**: Speech Synthesis Markup Language — XML-based markup for controlling TTS prosody, emphasis, pauses, and pacing
- **Segment**: A discrete section of the video script with its own narration, visual plan, and timing

## Requirements

### Requirement 1: Kokoro TTS Engine Integration

**User Story:** As a video creator, I want narration generated by Kokoro TTS, so that the voiceover sounds natural and professional rather than robotic.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the user selects Kokoro as the TTS engine, THE Pipeline SHALL generate narration audio using the Kokoro TTS API endpoint
2. THE Kokoro_TTS engine SHALL implement the TTSEngine interface (name, voices, generate, isAvailable methods) defined in the existing TTS registry
3. WHEN Kokoro_TTS is configured, THE TTS registry SHALL place Kokoro first in the fallback priority order (Kokoro → Grok → Melo → Browser)
4. THE Kokoro_TTS engine SHALL expose at least 4 voice options with distinct characteristics (e.g., male authoritative, female conversational, male dramatic, female professional)
5. WHEN Kokoro_TTS generation fails, THE TTS registry SHALL fall back to the next available engine and log the failure
6. THE Kokoro_TTS engine SHALL accept a server URL configuration parameter so users can point to a local or remote Kokoro instance
7. WHEN the Kokoro server is unreachable, THE Kokoro_TTS engine SHALL return null within 10 seconds to trigger fallback

### Requirement 2: Narration Pacing and Prosody Control

**User Story:** As a video creator, I want control over narration speed and emphasis, so that the voiceover matches the energy level of top YouTube videos (150–180 WPM).

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Kokoro_TTS engine SHALL accept a target WPM parameter between 120 and 200
2. WHEN generating narration for an intro segment, THE Kokoro_TTS engine SHALL use a WPM of 170–180 to create urgency
3. WHEN generating narration for an advice or outro segment, THE Kokoro_TTS engine SHALL use a WPM of 140–155 for clarity
4. THE Kokoro_TTS engine SHALL support SSML-like markup for emphasis on key phrases within narration text
5. WHEN a narration segment contains a statistic or data point, THE Kokoro_TTS engine SHALL apply a brief pause (300–500ms) before the data point for dramatic effect
6. THE NarrationStep component SHALL display the estimated WPM for each generated clip

### Requirement 3: Background Music Integration

**User Story:** As a video creator, I want background music layered under the narration, so that the video feels professionally produced and maintains viewer engagement.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Background_Music_Mixer SHALL layer a music track underneath the narration audio during video assembly
2. WHILE narration is active, THE Background_Music_Mixer SHALL reduce music volume to 15–20% of its peak level (ducking)
3. WHILE no narration is playing (between segments), THE Background_Music_Mixer SHALL restore music volume to 60–80% of peak level
4. THE Background_Music_Mixer SHALL apply a 500ms fade-in at the start and a 2-second fade-out at the end of the video
5. THE AssemblyStep component SHALL allow the user to select from at least 3 music mood presets (e.g., tense, uplifting, neutral)
6. WHEN the user disables background music via the toggle, THE Background_Music_Mixer SHALL produce output with narration only
7. THE Background_Music_Mixer SHALL crossfade music volume transitions over 200–400ms to avoid abrupt volume changes
8. THE Pipeline SHALL include at least 3 royalty-free music tracks bundled as static assets in the public/audio directory

### Requirement 4: Script Hook-First Structure

**User Story:** As a video creator, I want every script to open with a compelling hook in the first 30 seconds, so that viewers stay past the critical retention window.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN generating a script, THE Pipeline SHALL produce an intro segment that contains a hook within the first 2 sentences
2. THE hook SHALL use one of these patterns: surprising statistic, provocative question, personal-stakes statement, or counterintuitive claim
3. WHEN an LLM API key is available, THE Pipeline SHALL instruct the LLM to generate the hook using the pattern "Start with a surprising fact or statistic about [topic] that makes the viewer feel personally at risk"
4. WHEN no LLM API key is available, THE Pipeline SHALL use template-based hooks that embed the topic name and a generic urgency framing
5. THE intro segment narration SHALL be 40–60 words to fit within a 15–20 second spoken window at 170 WPM
6. THE ScriptStep component SHALL visually highlight the hook section with a distinct badge or color indicator

### Requirement 5: Enhanced Media Sourcing Quality

**User Story:** As a video creator, I want higher-quality media assets sourced for each segment, so that the video looks professional and avoids generic stock imagery.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Pipeline SHALL prefer images with resolution of 1920×1080 or higher for all video segments
2. WHEN a segment's visual plan specifies a human subject, THE media sourcer SHALL prioritize results containing people with clear facial expressions
3. WHEN the top-scored media candidate has a quality composite score below 100, THE Pipeline SHALL attempt a broadened search query before accepting the candidate
4. THE media sourcer SHALL reject candidates that match cliché visual patterns (hooded hacker, generic binary code, abstract circuit boards) when better alternatives score above 150
5. WHEN all sourced candidates for a segment score below 80, THE Pipeline SHALL generate a procedural background with the segment's semantic color palette rather than using a low-quality image
6. THE media sourcer SHALL attempt to source at least one video clip per 3 segments to add motion variety

### Requirement 6: Video Production — Transitions and Motion

**User Story:** As a video creator, I want professional transitions and motion effects between segments, so that the video feels dynamic rather than a slideshow.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE video renderer SHALL apply the section-appropriate transition (from SECTION_DESIGN_TEMPLATES) between consecutive segments
2. WHEN transitioning between segments of different section types, THE renderer SHALL use the motif transition defined for the outgoing section
3. THE renderer SHALL apply a Ken Burns effect (slow zoom or pan) to static images during their display duration
4. WHEN a segment contains a data point or statistic, THE renderer SHALL display an animated text card with the number for 2–3 seconds
5. THE renderer SHALL display section title cards (from generateSectionCard) for 1.2 seconds when the topic changes between sections
6. THE renderer SHALL maintain a minimum of 2 visual changes per 10-second window to prevent static frames

### Requirement 7: YouTube Metadata Generation

**User Story:** As a video creator, I want complete YouTube-ready metadata generated automatically, so that I can upload the video with optimized discoverability.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN video assembly completes, THE SEO_Metadata_Generator SHALL produce a title between 40 and 70 characters using the highest-CTR title option
2. THE SEO_Metadata_Generator SHALL generate a description containing: a 2–3 sentence summary, chapter markers with timestamps, and a tags line
3. THE SEO_Metadata_Generator SHALL produce 8–15 tags, each between 2 and 30 characters, derived from topic entities and style keywords
4. WHEN the script contains data points, THE SEO_Metadata_Generator SHALL embed at least one data point in the title
5. THE Pipeline SHALL display the generated title, description, and tags in the Preview step for user review and editing
6. THE SEO_Metadata_Generator SHALL generate chapter markers that align with segment start times in the rendered video

### Requirement 8: Thumbnail Generation Enhancement

**User Story:** As a video creator, I want a click-optimized thumbnail generated automatically, so that the video achieves high click-through rates on YouTube.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Thumbnail_Generator SHALL produce a 1280×720 image with a single dominant subject, bold text overlay (2–5 words), and high-contrast color accent
2. WHEN generating a thumbnail, THE Thumbnail_Generator SHALL select the highest-scored non-fallback media asset as the background image
3. THE Thumbnail_Generator SHALL apply a dark gradient overlay (40% top to 80% bottom opacity) to ensure text readability
4. THE text overlay SHALL use bold 52–56px font with a dark text shadow (blur 16–20px) for legibility at mobile thumbnail sizes (160×90px)
5. WHEN the script contains a hook line, THE Thumbnail_Generator SHALL derive the text overlay from the hook's key phrase rather than the generic title
6. THE Thumbnail_Generator SHALL generate 3 variant concepts (fear, curiosity, authority) and select the one with the highest visual hierarchy score
7. THE Preview step SHALL display the generated thumbnail with an option to regenerate or select a different variant

### Requirement 9: Narration Audio Export

**User Story:** As a video creator, I want narration exported as real audio files, so that the video assembly can mix narration with background music into a final output.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN Kokoro_TTS generates narration, THE Pipeline SHALL store the audio as a WAV or MP3 blob URL accessible to the assembly step
2. THE narration clips SHALL include timing metadata: start time offset, duration in seconds, and segment ID
3. WHEN all narration clips are generated, THE Pipeline SHALL calculate total narration duration and validate it falls within the target video duration (±20%)
4. IF total narration duration exceeds the target by more than 20%, THEN THE Pipeline SHALL warn the user and suggest removing or shortening segments
5. THE NarrationStep component SHALL display a playback button for each clip that plays the actual Kokoro-generated audio (not browser TTS)

### Requirement 10: Fast-Paced Editing Rhythm

**User Story:** As a video creator, I want the video to maintain a fast editing rhythm matching top YouTube content, so that viewers stay engaged throughout.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE renderer SHALL target a maximum visual hold time of 5 seconds per static image before applying a cut, zoom, or transition
2. WHEN a segment's narration exceeds 8 seconds, THE renderer SHALL split the visual into at least 2 shots with a cut or motion change at the midpoint
3. THE renderer SHALL alternate between close-up and wide-angle framing across consecutive segments to create visual variety
4. WHEN the video contains more than 5 segments, THE renderer SHALL insert at least 2 animated text cards (statistics, quotes, or section titles) distributed across the video
5. THE renderer SHALL ensure no single visual asset appears for more than 7 consecutive seconds without motion or overlay change
