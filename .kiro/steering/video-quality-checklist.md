# AI Video Generator Quality Checklist

This checklist defines the quality standards for AutoTube video generation. All pipeline stages (script generation, media sourcing, visual planning, rendering, and blind review) should reference these principles.

## Core Principles

1. Clarity first, then drama, then polish
2. Every second must have purpose — retention over completeness
3. Concrete consequences beat abstract concepts for broad audiences
4. Human faces and immediate consequences outperform generic tech imagery
5. Fear without a survival path reduces shareability

## Thumbnail Standards

- Must instantly communicate the topic in under 1 second
- One dominant subject, not competing focal points
- 2-5 words max text, readable on mobile
- Generate 3 concepts: fear version, curiosity version, authority/news version
- Use emotional contrast: calm victim vs digital danger
- Prefer face/object/screen close-ups over distant wide shots
- One accent color for urgency (red), rest restrained

## Hook (First 15 Seconds)

- First second must show danger, not setup
- Start with concrete risks: money, files, identity, business shutdown
- 3-5 fast visual beats before first explanatory sentence
- First line understandable by someone half-paying attention
- Within 15 seconds: viewer knows topic, stakes, and angle
- Intro should work even with audio low or muted
- Put strongest shot in first 5 seconds

## Script-to-Visual Alignment

- Every sentence must have a visual purpose
- Abstract lines need concrete visual translation
- Match visual scale to script scale (personal = personal visuals)
- Reject visuals that are technically relevant but emotionally weak
- Score image-to-line fit, not just keyword match

## Pacing Rules

- Cut on meaning shifts, not just time intervals
- Faster cuts in opener than explanation section
- Pattern interrupts every 20-30 seconds
- Build pacing in waves: impact → explanation → escalation → relief → impact
- Save one high-impact sequence for midpoint to prevent drop-off
- Dead air should be almost zero for under-5-minute videos

## Story Structure

- Open with immediate personal stakes
- Move from individual threat to larger system threat
- Bring viewer back to themselves regularly
- Each section answers one question before opening the next
- Limit big ideas introduced per minute
- End with agency, not pure fear

## Credibility

- Flag unsourced statistics for review
- Use "according to" language for major claims
- Distinguish verified claims from interpretive framing
- Emotional intensity must not come at expense of trust

## Scene Selection Logic

- Prioritize clips with clear human emotion
- Prioritize clips with obvious cause-and-effect
- Reject clips that are technically on-topic but visually vague
- Detect stock-footage fatigue risk
- Human-centered visuals outperform abstract tech backgrounds
- Consequences beat "hackers typing" imagery
- Every 15-20 seconds, add a shot that feels fresh

## Blind Review Scoring Dimensions

When evaluating a finished video, score on:
1. **Visual Quality** (1-10): Image clarity, composition, relevance, no watermarks
2. **Pacing** (1-10): Cut rhythm, pattern interrupts, momentum, no dead air
3. **Narrative Clarity** (1-10): Understandable without specialist knowledge, logical flow
4. **Thumbnail Effectiveness** (1-10): Instant comprehension, mobile readability, emotional hook
5. **Overall Production Value** (1-10): Audio quality, transitions, brand consistency, professional feel

Each score must include specific feedback explaining the rating with concrete examples from the video.
